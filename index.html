Our Story How it began, what we believe, and why we're here.

The Autism Dads Social Club began the same way many meaningful friendships begin — through unexpected encounters and shared experiences.

Two of us, Jonathan and Emmanuel, first met while dropping our children off at ABA therapy. After crossing paths several times, we discovered we lived in the same neighborhood. What started as casual conversations about our kids turned into discussions about family life, autism, and the challenges and joys of parenting. We even talked about getting our children together for a neighborhood bike ride. Then we kept talking. The other two of us, Jonathan and Jesse, met through our professional lives as historians. We both study civil rights history, and through our conversations we discovered another connection: we were both raising autistic children of similar ages. What began as academic friendship quickly became something deeper.

Eventually, all three of us connected over Zoom and began asking an important question:

How can we better support one another — and other fathers like us?

As we looked around, we noticed there were many spaces designed to support mothers. Yet fathers were often navigating the autism journey relatively alone. Many of us felt an unspoken expectation to stay strong, keep moving forward, and carry the weight quietly. We were expected to be steady, to be the rock, to keep our hands on the wheel.

But we also knew that dads need support, friendship, and community. So, in 2022, we founded the Autism Dads Social Club.

We did not want to create a traditional support group where we sit around in a circle. We wanted to build a brotherhood — an organization that offers meaning and joy to fathers on the journey.

ADSC is dads gathering for barbecue, bowling, fishing, golf, and conversation. It's autistic children playing freely while parents relax among people who understand. It's a text message from another father who's been there before. It's sharing resources, celebrating victories, offering encouragement, and showing up when someone needs support.

Most importantly, ADSC is the community we wish existed when our own children were first diagnosed. We can't eliminate every challenge, uncertainty, or difficult day. But we can make sure that no father feels alone.

Today, the Autism Dads Social Club is a growing community of fathers and families across the Houston area and beyond. We are united by a common belief:

Our autistic children are gifts to the world. In a society that too often focuses on limitations, we choose to celebrate possibility. We want our children to recognize their beauty, brilliance, dignity, and potential. We want fathers to know that they are not alone. And we want families to experience the power of genuine community.

Whether you are new to this journey or have been walking it for years, there is a place for you here.

You have a village. You have a brotherhood. You have a community willing to walk alongside you and your family.

Common questions

Things dads actually ask us.

Is this only for dads in Houston?
In-person meetups and family events are in the Houston-area (we run them everywhere from Sugar Land to Katy and Tomball). The newsletter and member text thread are open to dads anywhere. We have Charter members in and throughout Houston. Our Fatherhood Huddle Series is open to dads throughout the United States and to anyone who wants to join via Zoom.
What if my child is grown? Or recently diagnosed?
All dads who have a child on the spectrum, regardless of age, are welcome to attend an ADSC event. The focus isn't on the kid's age; it's on the experience of being a dad.
Is this a support group? A therapy thing?
No, nobody runs a session. It's a social club. We gather at pool halls, sports bars, barbecues, and bowling alleys. Sometimes, things happen that look like support because that's what happens when men actually talk. But the format is just hanging out.
Do I need to be religious? Any political affiliation?
No, on both counts. We are intentionally nondenominational and nonpolitical. We have members from every faith and no faith, every political background, and all kinds of households. The diagnosis doesn't consider any of that, and neither do we.
What does a meetup actually look like?
Mixers are typically held on the fourth Saturday of each month. Simply show up at the announced location around 3:00 p.m. and look for the table with the Autism Dads Social Club reserved sign. Light food and refreshments are usually provided, including items such as chicken wings, pizza, chips, and dip. Dads are invited to introduce themselves, share a little about their families, and participate in brief icebreakers designed to spark conversation and connection.

Some mixers also include fun activities such as driving range golf, billiards, bowling, darts, or other recreational outings. Throughout the gathering, announcements are shared about upcoming events, programs, and opportunities to get involved. Most importantly, mixers provide a relaxed environment where dads can build friendships, exchange resources, and connect with others who understand the special needs parenting journey.
Why pay for membership if events are free?
Great question. Membership helps make those free events possible. While many of our programs are offered at no cost, there are real expenses involved in running a nonprofit, including website hosting, insurance, food and refreshments, communications, marketing, and administrative services. Membership dues help sustain and grow our programs, support autism families, and ensure that dads can continue to access resources, events, and community. As a registered nonprofit, membership contributions are tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law.
How do I bring my family to a family event?
RSVP through the events page when it's announced. Tell us how many adults and kids, and the ages of the kids. There's no charge. We just need a head count for food and space. Spouses, partners, neurotypical siblings, grandparents — everyone is welcome.
Can I just come once and see how it feels?
Yes. That's how almost everyone starts. Come to one meetup. Buy your own beer. See what it's like. If it's not for you, no hard feelings, you're still on the newsletter. If it is for you, we'll see you next month. We will welcome you to become involved and invested in expanding our village and supporting the autism and special needs community.
Is the Autism Dads Social Club only for dads?
Not at all! While our name is Autism Dads Social Club, we welcome all male caregivers who play an important role in the lives of autistic children and adults to join our mixers. This includes fathers, grandfathers, uncles, stepfathers, godfathers, foster fathers, and other men who provide love, guidance, and support. We also create spaces for our families to mix and mingle. Our mission is simple: to unite fathers and families by building a community where men can learn, connect, and support one another — and where our families can socialize.
