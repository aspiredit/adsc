"Educate. Inspire. Motivate." This represents the foundation of Café Azul Tx Autism, a community organization established by Angel and Giset King to assist families navigating autism—particularly Spanish-speaking and bilingual populations. The organization grew from the founders' personal experience and provides resources alongside community connection and belonging.

### **Question 1: What inspired you to start Café Azul? How has your own autism parenting journey shaped the vision of your organization?**

Café Azul emerged from recognizing a critical gap: Spanish-speaking families needed accessible support and education in their language. A modest support group expanded into a nonprofit serving families across communities, eventually becoming bilingual. Both founders are educators who believe education empowers caregivers. When their daughter received an autism diagnosis, they experienced isolation despite attending training sessions. This prompted them to build an organization providing "community that educates, inspires, and motivates caregivers, ensuring they know they're not alone."

### **Question 2: What do you hope Café Azul offers to families and individuals in the autism community?**

The organization operates as more than a nonprofit—it functions as a supportive community. Café Azul provides sensory-friendly events, parent training, and community information while emphasizing one-on-one family support addressing specific challenges like potty training or ARD meetings. Training programs focus on nurturing entire families and helping caregivers develop resilience and self-care practices. The core belief: "no family should ever feel alone."

### **Question 3: What has been one of your most memorable or meaningful moments since opening?**

During the pandemic's first virtual conference, parents expressed feeling "seen, supported, and empowered." Additionally, Moms' Night Out events created meaningful spaces where participants connected and prioritized self-care—moments that reinforce the organization's mission.

### **Question 4: What message would you like to share with other families navigating the autism journey?**

Families should understand they're not isolated on this journey. The message emphasizes advocacy and celebrating "the beauty in the diversity around us." Children with special needs are "different, but they are not less." Like "a comforting cup of coffee brings warmth, energy, and focus," Café Azul aims to provide similar comfort to parents navigating special needs parenting.
