### Understanding Your Child's Rights

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) is federal legislation ensuring children with disabilities receive free and appropriate public education (FAPE). Schools must provide specialized instruction and related services tailored to each student's needs.

The Individualized Education Program (IEP) is a written document outlining specialized instruction and services. A team including parents, teachers, and school professionals develops this plan.

### Advocating for Your Child

- Stay informed about entitlements and rights
- Maintain regular communication with educators
- Participate actively in IEP meetings
- Document all school communications and concerns
- Seek professional advocacy support when needed

### Working with Your Child's School

- Build positive relationships with educators
- Share information about strengths and challenges
- Request regular progress updates
- Collaborate on intervention strategies
- Participate in school events and meetings
